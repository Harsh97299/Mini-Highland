How Big Does a Micro Mini Highland Cow Get?

June 15, 2025 Michael Smith 6 min read

The micro mini Highland cow has grown increasingly popular among small-farm owners, hobby farmers, and animal enthusiasts who love the classic fluffy Highland look but don’t have space for a full-size cow.

One of the most common questions we hear at MooMuffin Acres is simple but important: How big does a micro mini Highland cow actually get?

Quick Answer: A fully grown micro mini Highland cow typically stands 36–42 inches tall and weighs between 400–600 pounds.

What Is a Micro Mini Highland Cow?

A micro mini Highland cow is a selectively bred, smaller-framed version of the traditional Scottish Highland cow. They retain the long horns, thick shaggy coat, and calm temperament—just in a much more compact body.

  • Standard Highland cows
  • Mini Highland cows
  • Micro mini Highland cows

The difference comes down to height and mature weight.

How Big Does a Micro Mini Highland Cow Get?

Average Height

  • 36–42 inches (3–3.5 feet) at the shoulder
  • Measured at full maturity (3–4 years)

Average Weight

  • 400–600 pounds
  • Females usually stay lighter
  • Males may reach the upper range

For comparison, a standard Highland cow often weighs between 1,200–1,800 pounds.

Mini Highland Cow vs Micro Mini Highland Cow

Key Difference: Micro minis are noticeably shorter and lighter, making them better suited for small properties.

Feature Mini Highland Micro Mini Highland
Height 42–48 inches 36–42 inches
Weight 600–800 lbs 400–600 lbs
Space Needed Moderate Small

When Is a Micro Mini Highland Cow Fully Grown?

  • Reaches most height by 2 years
  • Fully fills out by 3–4 years
  • Slow growth is normal for heritage breeds

Important: Don’t judge adult size by calves—Highland cattle mature slowly.

How Much Space Does a Micro Mini Highland Cow Need?

  • ¼ to ½ acre per cow
  • Shelter from wind, rain, and heat
  • Secure fencing

Are Micro Mini Highland Cows Truly “Micro”?

There is no official global registry defining “micro mini.” Size depends heavily on genetics and breeder honesty.

  • Always ask for parent size
  • Avoid “teacup” marketing claims
  • Healthy cows should look sturdy, not fragile

Why Choose a Micro Mini Highland Cow?

  • Easier to handle
  • Lower feed costs
  • Fluffy, iconic appearance
  • Friendly temperament
  • Perfect for smaller farms

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a micro mini Highland cow the smallest Highland cow?
Yes, they are the smallest commonly bred Highland cattle.

Can they be kept as pets?
Yes, but they still require proper pasture and care.

How long do they live?
Typically 15–20 years.

Final Thoughts

If you love the look of Highland cattle but lack space, a micro mini Highland cow can be an excellent fit. Focus on ethical breeders, realistic size expectations, and long-term care—not just cuteness.
